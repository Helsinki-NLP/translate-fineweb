| Flip-flops, also called bistable gates, are digital logic circuits that can be in one of two states. Flip-flops maintain their state indefinitely until an input pulse called a trigger is received. When a trigger is received, the flip-flop outputs change state according to defined rules and remain in those states until another trigger is received. Flip-flop circuits are interconnected to form the logic gates for the digital integrated circuits (IC s) used in memory chips and microprocessors. Flip-flops can be used to store one bit, or binary digit, of data. The data may represent the state of a sequencer, the value of a counter, an ASCII character in a computer's memory or any other piece of information.<br/>There are several different kinds of flip-flop circuits, with designators such as T (toggle), S-R (set/reset) J-K (possibly named for Jack Kilby) and D (delay). A flip-flop typically includes zero, one, or two input signals as well as a clock signal and an output signal. Some flip-flops also include a clear input signal to reset the current output.<br/>The first electronic flip-flop was invented in 1919 by W. H. Eccles and F. W. Jordan. It used vacuum tubes and was initially called the Eccles-Jordan trigger circuit. | Flip-flopit, joita kutsutaan myös bistable porteiksi, ovat digitaalisia logiikkapiirejä, jotka voivat olla toisessa kahdesta tilasta. Flip-flop-piirit ovat yhteydessä toisiinsa muodostaen logiikkaportit digitaalisille integroiduille piireille (IC s), joita käytetään muistisiruissa ja mikroprosessoreissa. Flip-floppeja voidaan käyttää tallentamaan yksi bitti eli binääriluku datasta. Tiedot voivat edustaa sekvensserin tilaa, laskurin arvoa, tietokoneen muistissa olevaa ASCII-merkkiä tai mitä tahansa muuta tietoa.<br/>On olemassa useita erilaisia flip-flop-piirejä, joissa on tunnukset kuten T (toggle), S-R (set/reset) J-K (mahdollisesti nimetty Jack Kilbyn mukaan) ja D (delay). Flip-floppi sisältää tyypillisesti nolla-, yksi- tai kaksi tulosignaalia sekä kellosignaalin ja lähtösignaalin. The TransFly is also the only habitat of the New Guinea marsupial cat and the bronze quoll (Dasyurus spartacus).<br/>The TransFly is the richest area in New Guinea for freshwater turtles, with 9 of the 11 species occurring on the island living here. | Zeno formulated several mind-boggling questions, Zeno's paradoxes. They were a challenge to philosophers. For the most part, today they are considered to have been solved by the mathematical concept known as the limit.<br/>One of Zeno's paradoxes claims that it is impossible ever to reach a destination, such as a house that is a mile away. For in order to walk that mile, you must first walk half a mile, leaving half a mile to go. But before you can complete that remaining half a mile, you must first walk a quarter of a mile, leaving a quarter of a mile to go... and so on. Thus, apparently, you can get closer and closer and closer to your destination but can never actually quite get there. | Zeno muotoili useita ällistyttäviä kysymyksiä, Zenon paradokseja.
