There are two main types of fiber, soluble and insoluble. Soluble dissolves in water, while insoluble doesn’t. Fiber cannot be digested. However, soluble fiber is transformed as it passes through the digestive tract, where it is fermented by bacteria. Soluble fiber absorbs water, and becomes gelatinous as it does so. Insoluble fiber passes through the gut without changing its form. Quite simply, fiber promotes bowel movements and prevents constipation. | One of the things that I really like in a children’s book is that it teaches a lesson. The Hungry Fox by Cole Adams does that and much more. The story chronicles a fox’s journey to find food after waking up. Along the way, he makes many mistakes and learns a lesson each time. Each chapter is set up as an interaction with a different animal in the story and a different lesson.<br/>The book itself is visually striking. I love the illustrations. They are mostly in the style of a mosaic pieced together using different geometric shapes. It is a very unique style for illustrating a book. The colors are bright and engaging with a slightly fanciful air.<br/>The layout of the Table of Contents is as unique as the illustrations. It is set up as a map with the chapters laid out as way-points in a journey. Again, in place of what could be a droll portion of the book, the Table of Contents is creative and visually engaging.<br/>The Hungry Fox is written in the style of a fable. Talking animals that are used to teach a moral. In this case, each chapter teaches its own separate moral. In Chapter 1, the fox learns about greed. In Chapter 2, he learns about pride. Chapter 3 is a lesson on honesty. All in all, I counted seven different character traits/lessons in the book.<br/>There is some very high level vocabulary in the book. For example, in the chapter about greed, Adams uses the word avaricious to describe the fox. This is a word that many children would not be familiar with at all. The theme is evident throughout.<br/>If I had to label The Hungry Fox as any one genre, I would label it a fable. It has all the classic characteristics of a fable and reads like one as well. This lends itself very nicely to using it in a unit on fables and comparing it to the classic fables such as Anansi the Spider. Näin tehdessään se haastaa syvälle juurtuneet oletukset siitä, miten näemme itsemme, siirtymällä pelkästään itsemme näkemisestä "yksilöinä" kohti myös itsemme näkemistä osana maapalloa.
