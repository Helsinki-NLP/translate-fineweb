| Water heaters have become essential household equipment. Several household chores and conveniences like washing clothes and dishes or having a hot shower need hot water supplied by the water heater. It could be the first time that you are installing a water heater or replacing the old one.<br/>When installing a new water heater, you should first determine the capacity and energy consumption of the equipment. However, for both scenarios, you must know the different kinds of water heaters so that you get just what you want. It comes in different capacities varying from 30 gallons to 80 gallons. The heating system depends on the utility services available, and it can be anything like natural gas, oil, liquid propane, and electricity. The system uses a thermostat attached to the tank set at a given temperature. When the temperature exceeds the limit, the energy source is automatically cut off thereby preventing any possibilities of overheating. Again, when the temperature falls below a certain preset level, the heating system becomes operational. The cycle of heating the water in the tank continues round the clock and throughout the year.<br/>Half yearly cleaning of the tank is recommended to avoid deposition of sediment that can affect the performance. Besides, testing of the pressure relief valve is necessary to ensure its smooth functioning. Switch off the heating source when working on the water heater.<br/>Tankless water heaters<br/>As the name suggests, this type of water heater does not use any tank for storing water. Such types of equipment use the technology of instant heating and are also known as an instantaneous water heater. The water starts heating when you switch it on and is available for ready use. You get hot water as long as you keep the equipment switched on. The moment you switch it off, no more hot water will be available. Since heating is done when you need hot water, the system uses less energy as heating stored water does not require anything. This type of water heaters uses electricity as the source of energy as it draws heat from the environment first and then heats it up further for heating water in a tank. Although some might believe that it does not work well in all types of climate, the reality is just the opposite. The unit comprises an insulated tank and a solar collector and installing the equipment on the roof or in the yard is the standard practice. Active solar heaters use a pump to distribute water at different points while the passive solar heaters use the gravitational force. Active solar heaters can deploy direct or indirect circulation system for heating water. Passive solar heaters are less expensive and are available as integral collector storage system and thermosyphon system. | About the Telephone Consumer Protection Act<br/>The Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) was enacted by Congress in the early 1990s to protect consumers from unsolicited text messages, prerecorded and/or autodialed telephone calls (“robocalls”) and junk faxes.<br/>The TCPA is a powerful consumer privacy tool, it imposes fines of $500 for each violation and up to $1,500 for violations found to be willful and knowing.<br/>Who Does the TCPA Protect?<br/>The TCPA protects several different groups of consumers, including:<br/>Cell Phone Users: The TCPA protects cell phone users who receive unsolicited text messages, prerecorded solicitation calls and autodialed solicitation calls. Autodialed calls are often recognizable by a delay of a few seconds at the beginning of the call. The TCPA also protects individual cell phone users who receive solicitation calls (robocalls or live calls) when their number is registered with the National Do Not Call List.<br/>Residential Landline Users: The TCPA protects residential landline phone users who receive prerecorded solicitation calls. The TCPA also protects individuals who receive solicitation calls (robocalls or live calls) when their number is registered with the National Do Not Call List.<br/>Fax Machine Users (Including Businesses): The TCPA prohibits sending unsolicited fax advertisements.
