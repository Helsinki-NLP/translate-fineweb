| We live in a time of great observational advances in cosmology, which have given us a consistent picture of the matter and energy content of our Universe. Of the matter component, less than 2% is luminous, and no more than 20% is made of ordinary matter like protons, neutrons, and electrons. The rest of the matter component, more than 80% of the matter, is of an unknown form which we call non-baryonic. Finding the nature of non-baryonic matter is referred to as the non-baryonic dark matter problem.<br/>A summary of the current measurements of the matter density m and the energy density are shown in Figure 1 (adapted from Verde et al., 2002).
