Judith’s belt.<br/>The most elegant dress of a woman in the tenth century consisted of two tunics of different colors, one with long, the other with short sleeves; on the feet were boots laced up in front. Wide bands of embroidery bordered the throat, sleeves, and lower edge of the skirt.
