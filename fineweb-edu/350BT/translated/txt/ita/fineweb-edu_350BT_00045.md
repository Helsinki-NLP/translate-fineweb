For much of the period, Louisiana struggled for survival, and it never developed into the profitable, self-sustaining colony its founders had intended. French colonial Louisiana was more a “society with slaves” than a full-fledged “slave society,” a distinction commonly used by scholars who study the history of slavery in the United States. There were few enslaved people in Louisiana before 1720. Owners could free enslaved people only with the permission of the colony’s Superior Council.
