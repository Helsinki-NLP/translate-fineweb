Animal Products: The primary dietary sources of vitamin B12 are animal-based foods such as meat, fish, poultry, eggs, and dairy products.<br/>2. Fortified Foods: Some plant-based foods like breakfast cereals, plant-based milk alternatives, and nutritional yeast are fortified with synthetic vitamin B12.<br/>Causes of Vitamin B12 Deficiency:<br/>Several factors can contribute to a deficiency in vitamin B12. Understanding these causes is essential for preventing and addressing deficiencies:<br/>1. Inadequate Dietary Intake: A diet lacking in animal products or fortified foods can lead to B12 deficiency, especially in vegans and vegetarians.<br/>2. Malabsorption Disorders: Certain medical conditions like celiac disease, Crohn’s disease, atrophic gastritis, or pernicious anemia can interfere with the absorption of vitamin B12 in the digestive tract.<br/>3. Age: As people age, their ability to absorb B12 from food decreases, which can result in deficiency, particularly in older adults.<br/>4. Medications: Long-term use of certain medications, such as proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) and metformin, can hinder B12 absorption.<br/>5. Additionally, B12 deficiency can often be asymptomatic in its early stages or present with vague symptoms, so routine screening may be necessary for certain populations at risk, such as older adults, vegetarians, and individuals with gastrointestinal disorders.<br/>If you suspect you may have a B12 deficiency or are experiencing any of these symptoms, it’s essential to consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment, which typically involves dietary changes, B12 supplementation, or other medical interventions as needed. Dietary Modifications: Incorporate vitamin B12-rich foods into your diet, such as lean meats, fish, eggs, dairy products, and fortified foods if you’re a vegetarian or vegan.<br/>2. Supplements: In cases of severe deficiency or malabsorption disorders, healthcare providers may recommend B12 supplements in various forms, including pills, injections, or nasal sprays.<br/>3.
