| geography<br/><br/>Süderbrarup is located on the fishing peninsula . It is a sub-center and a recognized resort . In Süderbrarup there was a radium-containing spring, which was the destination of pilgrimages in pre-Christian times , but has dried up since the stadium was built.<br/><br/>history<br/><br/>The municipality has been inhabited since the Bronze Age, from which several burial mounds have been preserved. Particularly important, however, is the Kummerhy hill grave west of the Thorsberger Moors, which was first opened in 1861 and was built around 650 BC. BC and is surrounded by a stone circle. There are a total of 22 burial mounds in the area.<br/><br/>The Thorsberger Moor , from which many objects of daily use and weapons as well as decorative objects such as jewelry etc. a. were recovered, was - as the name suggests - consecrated to the Germanic god of war Thor as a place of sacrifice. It is assumed that Thorsberg was one of the most important places of worship in the region - the fact that the old name Thorsberg was not forgotten even in Christian times should indicate this. Most of the finds are kept in the Schleswig-Holstein State Museum in Gottorf Castle. About 2000 urns from the period up to AD 400 were excavated on the market square. The large number of used, jagged weapons that were sunk in the bog in the 4th century is interpreted as an indication of fighting that took place in Jutland during this time and may be related to the migration of the peoples . It is conceivable that the military conflicts were important in the context of the emigration of most of the Angles to Britain .<br/><br/>The place Süderbrarup was first mentioned in 1231 as Syndräbrathorp ( brathorp : village on a slope). On the other side of the Oxbek there is accordingly also Norderbrarup , which was the more important place until the 19th century. Belşaţar a rămas în Babilon pentru a ţine capitala, dar în schimb a ţinut un ospăţ beat în timp ce tatăl său a mărşăluit trupele sale spre nord pentru a-l întâlni pe Cirus. Două zile mai târziu, armatele lui Cirus cel Mare au răsturnat Babilonul.<br/><br/>VII. Belşaţar 542 (?) - 539 î.Hr.: Conform Bibliei, Belşaţar era regele regal în timpul căderii lui<br/>Babilon, ultimul rege al Imperiului Babilonian. Daniel îl numește ca fiul lui Nebucadnețar în Dan 5: 22. (Acest lucru nu înseamnă neapărat că el este un descendent direct, totuși. Cercetătorii biblici sunt ei înșiși divizați în ceea ce privește descendența lui, unii îl numesc nepotul lui Nebucadnețar, iar alții spun că el și Nabonid sunt una și aceeași. Istoricul evreu Iosephus, în Antichitățile evreilor, spune că Belșațar a fost un alt nume pentru Nabonidus.) Majoritatea istoricilor nu îl înregistrează ca fiind vreodată rege, totuși, numind Nabonidus ca ultimul rege al Imperiului Roman.<br/>Imperiul Neo-Babilonian.<br/><br/>Indiferent de confuzia care poate exista cu privire la numele sau si regalitatea, istoricul grec Herodot confirma ca ospata de beata gazduita de Belshazzar (in al treilea an al domniei sale, potrivit lui Daniel, cap. 5), a avut loc la 12 octombrie 539 i.Hr., si ca Babilonul a cazut in acea noapte armatelor medo-persane din Egipt.<br/>Fiica lui Belşaţar, Vasti, strănepoata regelui Nebucadneţar, s-a căsătorit mai târziu cu<br/>Ahaşveroş (Xerxes), fiul lui Darius cel Mare |
