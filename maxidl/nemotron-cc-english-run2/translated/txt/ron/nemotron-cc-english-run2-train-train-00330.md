This would make flying more arduous and induce the bird's head to tip upward, problems easily avoided by collapsing the crest.<br/><br/>It took me a long time before I noticed the pattern whereby a crested bird systematically collapses its crest during flight. Why was this pattern so difficult to spot? • First, much of the difficulty seems to come from the problem of spotting a behavioural negative: a crest not erected.
