Heat your iron on medium. Take your iron and place it directly on top of the press cloth for 5 seconds, lift the iron and repeat until all of the creases have disappeared. Do not drag your iron across the fabric.<br/><br/>Step 3 – Cutting Your Fabric<br/><br/>Use the measurements from "Making A Pillowcase: Part 1" to cut out 1 piece of fabric. Your machine may be different from mine so refer to your machine's owner manual.<br/><br/>Step 8 – Positioning Your Fabric<br/><br/>Set up your machine for an overcasting stitch. Lift the presser foot and position the edge of your fabric against the presser foot guide (this is the right side of the presser foot). Do not pull on the fabric. The machine will automatically do this for you. Your job is to guide the fabric on a straight path. Use a gentle hand. Let the machine do most of the work. Do this for 1 long side and 1 short side. Always lift the needle before you lift the presser foot. Always put the presser foot down before you put the needle down. Your overcasting stitch should look similiar to the image below.<br/><br/>Step 10 – Changing Stitches<br/><br/>Change the setting on your machine for a top stitch. Begin with a backstitch. Then continue the rest until you are close to the edge of your seam allowance. Stop!<br/><br/>Step 11 – Turning The Fabric<br/><br/>At this point, the needle should be in the fabric. If you are not on your mark manually stitch by moving the knob towards you 2-3 times until you reach the margin and the needle is barely in the fabric. Lift the pressure foot and turn your fabric. The needle should be holding the corner in place<br/><br/>Step 12 – Continue Sewing<br/><br/>Continue until you are close to the end but not past it. Finish with another backstitch.<br/><br/>Step 13 – Making The Hem<br/><br/>Fold the hem back to the desired length. Make sure that your pillow still fits and the line is even. Press with an iron until you get a good crease.<br/><br/>Step 14 – Securing The Hem<br/><br/>Secure the hem 5 mm from the edge with a top stitch. Make sure the needle is lowered into the fabric before you start.<br/><br/>Step 15 – Sewing & Back-stitching<br/><br/>Start sewing using the sewing guide to keep a straight line. End with a couple of back-stitches.
