Contrary to some depictions of "the Great Pacific Garbage Patch," the problem is not so much a floating landfill of toys and bottles, rather it's an abundance of tiny degraded bits of plastic that a variety of organisms ingest.<br/><br/>Plastic undergoes chemical reactions and a physical beating in the ocean that reduces it to specks. To make a real difference, you have to attack the problem closer to the source—when the plastic is still largely intact and before it reaches the ocean.<br/><br/>At beaches or bends in waterways where plastic waste tends to accumulate, plastic items are still in good enough shape that they could actually be recycled.
