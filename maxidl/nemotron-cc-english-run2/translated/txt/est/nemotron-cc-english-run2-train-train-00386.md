It produces progressive weakness in all muscles, including those involved in the defecation process, such as the abdominal muscles.<br/><br/>Decreased fluid intake. Older people, due to alteration of the thirst center, located in the central nervous system , do not feel thirsty and stop drinking enough fluids.<br/><br/>Decreased fiber intake in the diet.<br/><br/>Having irregular bowel habits: When the need for defecation is not responded to, the stool becomes more compact.<br/><br/>The use of laxatives and enemas continuously causes the intestine to require increasingly higher and more frequent doses of these drugs to achieve the same effect. They are responsible for chronic constipation.<br/><br/>Environmental situations such as lack of privacy to carry out the deposition.
