In fact, it is ineffective in treating JIA in around 35% of all patients. Methotrexate is also cytotoxic, and can cause liver damage for which regular blood tests are required.<br/><br/>Where children and young people are unable to take methotrexate, they may move on to 'biologics' – the next level of drugs. These work in a variety of ways, but all work to reduce the immune system.<br/>As a result of having their immune system reduced, children and young people on anti-JIA medications are more likely to contract illnesses. Even relatively simple illnesses can become severe and life-threatening.<br/><br/>Prospects for children and young people with JIA<br/><br/>Around half of all children and young people with JIA will go into remission within ten years.
