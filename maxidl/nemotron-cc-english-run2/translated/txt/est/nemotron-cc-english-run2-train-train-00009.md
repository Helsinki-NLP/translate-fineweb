The Nile was very predictable. Every year, it would flood at about the same time. The floods were not typically destructive. Instead, they actually brought life to the land. Historians argue that this influenced the Egyptians and led them to believe that the cosmic world was orderly and relatively benign as well. The Egyptians believed in the idea of Ma'at, a fixed, eternal order for the universe. They believed that Ma'at was under attack and needed to be sustained by their offerings and rituals. In other words, they had a world view that emphasized order and routine, just as the Nile acted in orderly ways.
