Insulin is a protein and that means it is made up of amino acid building blocks. Depending on the type and arrangement of the amino acids, the insulin molecule forms different 3-D shapes. There are a variety of insulin molecules. Cat insulin is considerably different from human insulin.<br/><br/>So what if they don't look the same? Well, they don't ACT the same, either. So, let's look some more at these characteristics and then compare some different insulins. Simple graphs are the best way to make the comparisons.<br/><br/>Onset of action simply means when the insulin starts working to lower the blood sugar. This takes some time!
