If you send in two bits, the OR output will be the maximum of those two bits, and the AND output will be the minimum of those two inputs. (Just check all 4 cases, if it isn't obvious that that is true).<br/><br/>Now look up sorting circuits in Knuth. Since his circuits use nothing but 2-input sorting functions, and since each of them is a monotone circuit, the entire thing will be a monotone circuit.<br/><br/>Now it's easy to build a majority circuit. That's the majority.<br/><br/>The first known example of a monotone function whose circuit reduces from exponential size to polynomial size by using NOT gates was more complicated than a simple majority function.
