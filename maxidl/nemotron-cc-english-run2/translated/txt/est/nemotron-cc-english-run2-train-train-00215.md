| When we need to justify art<br/><br/>There are two types of art. Art that emerges from creativity versus art that's intended to sell.<br/><br/>The first type is generally arbitrary. How it's graded though depends on its freshness. If we've seen art like it before we'll probably not be that intrigued.<br/><br/>The second type of art is designed commercially. It most likely contains a figure that alludes a specific message about a product. When we see this art, we don't even call it art; we call it marketing.<br/><br/>Whatever type of art it is, it ultimately gets judged by familiarity and experience. True art is something instinctual; an assessment made after years of studying and seeing other works. One can tell immediately if a piece stands out.<br/><br/>Commercial art, on the other hand, always needs tweaking; it means different things to different people. Grading is not done via gut check but rather a consensus.<br/><br/>Real art can't be justified; you either "get it" or you don't. The immediate understanding takes years of experience.
