There are no cars or paved roads, but over 18 miles of panoramic, rugged, and mostly coastal walking trails.<br/><br/>Geology<br/><br/>Acadia is renowned for the natural beauty of its panoramic views and pink granite mountains. The landscape speaks to a long history of glacial, tectonic and volcanic activity. The story continues today but in a gentler way, as erosion and the elements continue to carve new surroundings. The varied landscape of Acadia morphs through the weathering of granite ridges, the relentless flow of tides, and gusting coastal wind. Nature doesn't disappoint here, it's what helps bring visitors back year after year.<br/><br/>When to Visit<br/><br/>The high season in Acadia is the summer, May through October. If you're an early riser, solitude can be found by visiting popular spots earlier in the day, or by venturing off the beaten path and opting for less popular itineraries.<br/><br/>Springtime sees more wind, precipitation, and muddy trails.
