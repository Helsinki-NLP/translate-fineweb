One Hertz is the equal to one sound wave or cycle per second. The more frequent the sound waves, the higher the pitch, and the higher the Hertz number. This frequency range is critical for hearing and understanding speech and other sounds, and is the reason routine hearing screening uses 500 Hz, 1000 Hz, 2000 Hz, 4000 Hz, and 6000 Hz (ages 11 and older).<br/><br/>Volume (intensity or loudness) is the range of sound loudness measured in decibels (dB). The greater the decibel number, the louder the sound. The minimal sound level that the majority of people with normal hearing can detect is 0dB.
