Altogether, that puts Goose Island a stone's throw from being a sub-brand of the largest brewer in the United States. I've had a lot of beers in recent years, and this is the only one of the lot that wears a tux. This, too, is the sort of stuff that pleases beer review sites.<br/><br/>The only problem comes after I've been drinking for awhile. About halfway through the bottle, I begin to notice an odd sliminess. It's difficult to describe - a sort of grimy feeling coating my mouth. It's not pleasant, but that's not enough to keep me from finishing the thing.<br/><br/>As an oatmeal stout, then, this is quite good: it's smooth, it's fairly light, it smells fantastic, and I could probably drink it all night if it weren't for that slimy buildup.
