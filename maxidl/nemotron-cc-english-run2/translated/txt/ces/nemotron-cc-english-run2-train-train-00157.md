Our planet is already beginning to warm, and with that warming comes extreme storm events, rapid spread of diseases, sea level rise, loss of biodiversity…the list goes on. And these are just the effects climate scientists can currently predict. Such a rapid change in climate worldwide will have far-reaching, unforeseeable impacts. The question becomes, how can we adapt our buildings and cities to this new, unpredictable world?<br/><br/>Of course, the first step is to stop the main cause of this rapid change: greenhouse gas emissions. Many efforts are already well underway with the hope to drastically curb our emissions. Yet, even if all emissions were halted tomorrow, the climate would still warm. We must prepare for the consequences. After storms like Hurricane Sandy and Katrina, many designers, planners, and policymakers are discussing solutions to cope with sea level rise, coastal flooding, and storm surge events.
