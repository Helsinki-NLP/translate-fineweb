Planned sequences of study in grades 9-12, 10-12 or 11-12 are closely aligned with two years of postsecondary study in a technical field, in associate degree or apprenticeship programs. All Perkins Title II funded programs are required to have seven key elements:<br/><br/>an articulation agreement between secondary and postsecondary consortium participants;<br/><br/>an instructional design that aligns a grade 9-12, 10-12 or 11-12 sequence of study with two years of postsecondary instructional design with a common core of proficiency in math, science, communication, and technology;<br/><br/>a specifically developed Perkins Title II curriculum;<br/><br/>joint in-service training of secondary and postsecondary teachers to implement the Perkins Title II curriculum effectively;<br/><br/>training of counselors to recruit students and to ensure program completion and appropriate employment;<br/><br/>equal access for special populations to the full range of Perkins Title II programs;and<br/><br/>preparatory services such as recruitment, career and personal counseling, and career assessment. | Perkins Title II Přehled<br/><br/>Perkins hlava II financuje partnerství místních vzdělávacích agentur (LEA), BOCES a postsekundárních vzdělávacích institucí pro rozvoj a provoz programů CTE, které se skládají z nejméně posledních dvou let středního vzdělávání a nejméně dvou let postsekundárního vzdělávání.
