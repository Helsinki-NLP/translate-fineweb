| Laser cutting machines have brought about efficiency and precision in the metal fabrication industry as compared to the days when mechanical cutting was the only available option. The machine is ideal when cutting into thick metals as it can sustain the required cutting speed. In the case of fier laser cutting machines, the cutting speed is slower when using a thick metal. Also, the finished product is not as smooth as is when using CO2 laser cutters.<br/><br/>The downside of CO2 laser cutters is that they consume a lot of power compared to fiber laser cutters; therefore, you may end up incurring more for energy costs.<br/><br/>Fiber laser cutting machine<br/><br/>Fiber is one of the latest innovations in the laser cutting industry and is by far more energy and time efficient as compared to the use of CO2 cutting. Fiber laser cutting machines are usually ideal for thin metals if you want to achieve a smooth surface and excellent cutting speed. One of its advantages is the ability to cut into reflective metals such as aluminium, copper, and brass without back reflections and damage to the machine.<br/><br/>On the downside, fiber laser cutting is not efficient when used with thick metals; hence, it is not recommended.<br/><br/>Determine the speed of the machine<br/><br/>Laser cutting machines operate at different speeds, and you want to ensure that the one you choose is suited for your metal cutting speed requirements. High-speed cutting machines are always preferable as you can adjust the speed depending on the thickness of the metal for a smooth cut.<br/><br/>Choose the ideal power consumption<br/><br/>Laser cutting machines can be classified according to their wattage. High-wattage machines are ideal for laser cutting of stainless steel, aluminium, brass, and titanium among other metals. For thin metals, go for a machine with average wattage.
