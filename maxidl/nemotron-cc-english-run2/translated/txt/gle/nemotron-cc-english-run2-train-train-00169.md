| All About Feral Cats<br/><br/>What is a Feral Cat?<br/><br/>Feral cats are born and raised without human contact or have been abandoned and reverted to wild ways in order to survive. Ferals often live in groups, called colonies, and take refuge wherever they can find a food source. They also try to seek out abandoned buildings or deserted cars to escape the elements. Unmanaged feral cat colonies are the most underserved segment of companion animals, resulting in extreme overpopulation with dreadful consequences.<br/><br/>Why are there Feral Cats?<br/><br/>"Feral cats are the offspring of lost or abandoned pet cats or other feral cats who are not spayed or neutered. Females can reproduce two to three times a year, and their kittens, if they survive, will become feral without early contact with people. Cats can become pregnant as early as 5 months of age, and the number of cats rapidly increases without intervention by caring people." ~ Humane Society<br/><br/>How do Feral Cats survive?<br/><br/>"Many don't survive. If they do survive, their lives aren't easy without human caretakers. Females may become pregnant as young as 5 months of age and may have two to three litters a year. Being pregnant so young and so often, and having and nursing kittens, is even more stressful on female cats who are struggling to survive. More than half of the kittens are likely to die without human intervention. Males who roam and fight to find mates and defend their territories may be injured and transmit diseases to one another through bite wounds. Feral cats may find food in a restaurant dumpster or someone may feed them. They may find shelter from the elements beneath a porch or in an abandoned building. But often they are without a reliable source of food and adequate shelter. Trap-Neuter-Return is a non-lethal strategy to reduce the number of feral cats and improve the quality of life for cats, birds, wildlife, and people." ~ Humane Society<br/><br/>What is Trap, Neuter, and Return (TNR)?<br/><br/>Trap-Neuter-Return (TNR) is the most humane and effective way of controlling feral cat populations. It has been used successfully in the US, England and Denmark, and in other forward-thinking countries. At a minimum, feral cats who are TNR'ed are spayed/neutered so they can no longer reproduce, vaccinated, and surgically ear-tipped on one ear (usually the left and is the universally-recognized sign of a cat who has been TNRed). Dedicated caretakers feed and provide shelter for TNRed cats, monitor the cats for illness, and remove new cats for TNR and tame ones for adoption. TNR eliminates the objectionable behavior of unsterilized animals that includes fighting and wandering (looking for mates). It not only controls the unchecked growth of unsterilized cats, but improves the health, behavior and quality of life of the homeless animals.<br/><br/>How Do I Schedule a Neuter/Spay Appointment?<br/><br/>Divine Feline and The Feline Fix clinic is a local Denver Organization that provides low cost spay/neuter cat surgeries. In The Lancet, Iria Grande and colleagues1<br/>present a timely and informative overview of the many<br/>clinical manifestations of bipolar disorder and consider,<br/>in particular, its long-term management, highlighting<br/>the important part that lithium still plays.<br/>With respect to diagnosis, accurate assessment of<br/>bipolar symptoms requires a thorough and advanced<br/>approach that carefully integrates cross-sectional and<br/>longitudinal clinical information.1 However, even with<br/>skilled, structured, clinical interviewing, the diagnostic<br/>consistency of bipolar disorder in the long term cannot<br/>be guaranteed and, over the course of a decade, as many<br/>as 20% of patients initially diagnosed with the illness<br/>will acquire an alternative diagnosis.2 Conversely, up to<br/>a fifth (3·3–21·6%) of patients in primary care diagnosed<br/>with major depression might have undiagnosed bipolar<br/>disorder.3 Thus, in clinical practice, the misdiagnosis of<br/>bipolar disorder is common. One of the key reasons for<br/>such diagnostic inconsistency is that the boundaries<br/>of the illness are blurred. For example, the upper and<br/><br/>lower cutoffs for bipolar II disorder are wholly arbitrary,<br/>and criteria for duration and severity of symptoms lack<br/>biological significance.4<br/>Clinically, the intricate nature of bipolar disorder also<br/>complicates diagnosis. For instance, mild presentations<br/>are usually difficult to distinguish from normal mood<br/>fluctuations and features of personality, particularly<br/>in the early stages of the illness. But even the typical<br/>clinical picture of bipolar disorder is, for the most part,<br/>distorted by comorbid anxiety or development of mixed<br/>states. Mixed states are characterised by coterminous<br/>symptoms of depression and mania, and do not<br/>conform with present-day taxonomy.5 All these factors<br/>make diagnosis of bipolar disorder very difficult, and the<br/>process is even more complicated in adolescence, when<br/>the brain is still developing.<br/>Hence, the diagnosis of bipolar disorder has been at<br/>the centre of recent controversy,6 particularly in children,<br/>in whom the illness is referred to variably as paediatric<br/>bipolar disorder, juvenile bipolar disorder, or early-onset<br/>bipolar disorder. Maidir le neamhord bipolar, is féidir leis an dá phróiseas dúshláin shuntasacha a chruthú. Tá an tinneas thar a bheith casta agus intuartha, agus déantar diagnóis agus cóireáil a chumasc leis an gcomhthéacs ina dtáirgeann an neamhord. Sa Lance, iria Grande agus i chomhghleacaithe1<br/>léargas tráthúil agus faisnéiseach a thabhairt ar go leor léirithe cliniciúla a bhfuil neamhord bipolar orthu agus a mheas, go háirithe a bhainistiú go fadtéarmach, ag cur béime ar an gcuid thábhachtach a imríonn litiam fós.<br/>Maidir le diagnóis, teastaíonn measúnú cruinn ar chomharthaí bipolar cur chuige críochnúil agus fadaimseartha a chomhtháthaíonn faisnéis chliniciúil thrasearnála agus fadaimseartha.1 Mar sin féin, fiú le hoilte, struchtúrtha, cumarsáid chliniciúil, comhsheasmhacht dhiagnóiseach an neamhord bipolar san fhadtéarma, ní féidir í a ráthú agus, le linn deich mbliana, an oiread agus is 20% d’othair a ndearnadh diagnóis mhalartach orthu.2 ar bhonn réasúnta, suas le cúigiú (3·21·6%) d’othair a ndearnadh diagnóis orthu i gcúram príomhúil, d’fhéadfadh neamhord bipolar go ndéanfaí diagnóis orthu.3 Dá bhrí sin, i gcleachtas cliniciúil, is coitianta. Ceann de na príomhchúiseanna le neamhréireacht dhiagnóiseach den sórt sin ná go bhfuil teorainneacha an tinnis doiléir, mar shampla, an uachtair agus<br/><br/>tá gearradh níos ísle do neamhord bipolar II go hiomlán treallach, agus tá tábhacht bhitheolaíoch ag baint le critéir ar feadh ré agus déine na hairíonna.4<br/>Go cliniciúil, an nádúr casta de neamhord bipolar chomh maith leis sin tá diagnóis casta. Mar shampla, is gnách go mbíonn sé deacair ar láithreoireachtaí éadroma idirdhealú a dhéanamh ó ghnáth-lamhnuithe giúmar agus gnéithe pearsantachta, go háirithe i gcéimeanna tosaigh an tinneas. Ach fiú an pictiúr cliniciúil tipiciúil an neamhord bipolar, den chuid is mó, arna shaobhadh ag imní cobróideach nó forbairt stáit measctha. Tá comharthaí coitinnithe de dúlagar agus mania, agus níl siad ag teacht le tacsanomy.5 Déanann na fachtóirí seo diagnóis ar neamhord bipolar an-deacair, agus tá an próiseas níos casta fós san ógántacht, nuair a bhíonn an inchinn ag forbairt fós.<br/>Dá réir sin, tá diagnóis neamhord bipolar i lár an chonspóide le déanaí, 6 go háirithe i leanaí, ina ndéantar tagairt don bhreoiteacht mar neamhord bipolar péidiatraice, neamhord bipolar júfín, nó neamhord bipolar luath-oinse.
