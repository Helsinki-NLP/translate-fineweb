Alternatively, it can be structured as a "springing" power, becoming active only when a particular event or condition, such as the principal's mental incapacity, occurs.<br/><br/>Can I specify which financial decisions my agent can make?<br/><br/>Yes, you can tailor the SFPOA to grant specific powers, like managing particular bank accounts or handling real estate transactions. Conversely, you can provide a broad authority. If the document is labeled "durable," it remains in effect even if the principal becomes incapacitated.<br/><br/>How can I revoke an SFPOA?<br/><br/>To revoke an SFPOA, the principal must be mentally competent and provide a written notice to the agent.
