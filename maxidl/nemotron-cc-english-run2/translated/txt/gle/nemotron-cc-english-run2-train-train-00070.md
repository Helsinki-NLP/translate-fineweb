The first layer is the endometrium and responds to hormonal changes. This is the layer of the uterus that is shed during menstruation. The myometrium, the middle layer, is made up of mostly smooth muscle and is the thickest part of the uterus. It gives the uterus the ability to push a fetus out. The final layer is the perimetrium, which is a thin layer of tissue that envelops the uterus.<br/><br/>Uteran Prolapse<br/><br/>Normally, the uterus is held up by muscles, tendons, and ligaments.
