| I'm pretty sure that most if not all airports have pax boarding from the left and aircraft servicing performed on the right, ie baggage, fuel, catering etc. The most logical reason is that airlines/airports don't want pax in this area because the space is required for the above tasks to be carried out. Some pax may even end up in the hold (they get everywhere ) Hence all service related tasks are kept to the right and pax are boarded/disembarked from the left.<br/><br/>That said, I have seen pics of airports in the past where the right has been used for pax boarding but this is very unusual.<br/><br/>At a guess I would say it goes back to shipping days. Ships dock on the left (port-side) which is where we also get "port" from. There's no real reason why planes cannot be docked on the right however some aircraft manufactures keep to the rule so tend to have smaller "service" doors on the right e.g 757, 737 etc. most large aircraft eg 747 and 777s have normal sized doors on the starboard (right) so the only thing stopping boarding there is the airport infrastructure.<br/><br/>Caterers use the starboard side of the aircraft and generally cargo doors are placed there.<br/><br/>Tradition, nothing more really: Skipper sits at port side, passenger embark at port side.
