When first meeting with a potential BCBA, be upfront and honest regarding your child's current skills, deficits, and challenges. For the BCBA to determine if she or he has the education, training, and experience necessary to help your child, the BCBA will need to conduct an assessment. It is unethical for the BCBA to agree to take a case if she or he does not have the skills needed to perform competently.
