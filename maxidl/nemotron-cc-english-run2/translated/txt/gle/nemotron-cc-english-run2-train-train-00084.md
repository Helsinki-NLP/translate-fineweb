| UK: Bribery Act 2010 - What's In It For Me?<br/><br/>The Bribery Act 2010 received Royal Assent on 8 April 2010. The test is the<br/>intention of the bribe, not the outcome.<br/><br/>Being bribed<br/><br/>On the other side of the transaction, if it can be called that,<br/>a person is also guilty of an offence when (summarising) that<br/>person requests, agrees to receive, or accepts a financial or other<br/>advantage as a reward for improper performance, or where a person<br/>makes improper performance anticipating financial or other<br/>advantage.<br/><br/>Effect of Bribery<br/><br/>The offences of bribing or being bribed can be committed in<br/>relation to many activities:<br/><br/>functions of a public nature;<br/><br/>any activity connected with a business;<br/><br/>any activity performed in the course of a person's<br/>employment; and<br/><br/>any activity performed by or on behalf of a body of persons<br/>(corporate or not).<br/><br/>For an offence to be committed, though, the person performing<br/>the function or activity must:<br/><br/>be one who is expected to perform the function or activity in<br/>good faith and/or<br/><br/>be one who is expected to perform the function or activity<br/>impartially; or<br/><br/>be in a position of trust.<br/><br/>The concept of improper performance is at the core of the Act.<br/>The test of improper performance turns substantially on the<br/>standard of performance which may normally be expected.
