| Constipation<br/><br/>The constipation may be defined as the passage of stool in small amounts, hard, dry, or absence of deposition for a certain period of time, generally greater than several days.<br/><br/>For its part, fecal impaction is understood to be the accumulation of hard stools in the rectal ampulla and in the distal portion of the large intestine with impossibility of its expulsion.<br/><br/>Both situations represent an important health problem in the elderly due to their biological and socio-sanitary repercussions.<br/><br/>Causes of constipation<br/><br/>There are several circumstances that favor constipation and fecal impaction. The most important ones are:<br/><br/>Decreased physical activity or mobility disorders, especially in bedridden people. It produces progressive weakness in all muscles, including those involved in the defecation process, such as the abdominal muscles.<br/><br/>Decreased fluid intake. Older people, due to alteration of the thirst center, located in the central nervous system , do not feel thirsty and stop drinking enough fluids.<br/><br/>Decreased fiber intake in the diet.<br/><br/>Having irregular bowel habits: When the need for defecation is not responded to, the stool becomes more compact.<br/><br/>The use of laxatives and enemas continuously causes the intestine to require increasingly higher and more frequent doses of these drugs to achieve the same effect. They are responsible for chronic constipation.<br/><br/>Environmental situations such as lack of privacy to carry out the deposition. For example: elderly people who are hospitalized or who share the same room with other people.<br/><br/>Dementias (one of the manifestations is a decreased response to defecation).<br/><br/>Changes in the digestive system typical of age: decreased intestinal motility, atrophy of the intestinal mucosa and decreased secretions and alterations in intestinal innervation.<br/><br/>Symptoms of constipation<br/><br/>It is characterized by the following facts:<br/><br/>Decreased frequency of bowel movements.<br/><br/>Hard and dry stools.<br/><br/>Effort to perform the deposition.<br/><br/>Feeling of fullness and / or bloating.<br/><br/>Loss of appetite<br/><br/>Diffuse abdominal pain, sometimes.<br/><br/>How is it diagnosed?<br/><br/>Due to the clinical manifestations described above.
