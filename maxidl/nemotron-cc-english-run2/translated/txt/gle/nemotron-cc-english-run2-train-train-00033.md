| Arnold Potosky, PhD, discusses the social and environmental factors that effect patient care.<br/><br/>July 16, 2010<br/><br/>Arnold Potosky, PhD, director of Health Services at Lombardi believes that there are two worlds when it comes to reducing the burden of cancer: the world of laboratories and clinical trials, and the "real world", where patients receive care.<br/><br/>"All too often cancer patients do not receive the best possible care, due to the barriers that exist in the health care delivery system," says Potosky. As the director of Health Services Research, his research focuses on what happens in this real world of cancer care delivery, specifically, the patterns of care, and patient outcomes such as survival and quality of life. This information can be used to advise patients faced with decisions about their care, with the ultimate goal of improving decision making and the quality of care.<br/><br/>"Personalized medicine is a rapidly emerging concept in cancer care and health care more generally," says Potosky. This type of medicine is commonly held to include tailoring cancer therapies based mainly on a person's genetic make-up and tumor characteristics, but he stresses that personalization should also account for other personal and environmental risk factors as well as cultural values and preferences. He stresses that many factors can contribute to disease outcomes, including psychological, social, economic, and cultural factors.<br/><br/>"The quality of care delivered in the community is the result of more than just a patient's biology," he says. Potosky is hopeful that doctors and policy makers are increasingly aware that this combination of factors has a critical effect on patients' survival and well-being, and that health care reform and more research studies will incorporate this broader view of medicine.<br/><br/>Potosky studies the effectiveness and side effects of prescribing hormonal therapy for prostate cancer. The long term benefits of this therapy remain unclear, but serious risks include bone fractures, heart problems, and diabetes. Potosky hopes to clearly communicate these risks and benefits to the patients and their doctors, so each man can make a personal choice.<br/><br/>In his other research, Potosky conducts surveys about patient well-being and experiences after cancer treatment. Standardized ways of asking patients to report their experiences with symptoms and other aspects of their quality of life are known as "patient reported outcome measures." However personal factors such as socioeconomic status, cultural background, age and race may influence how patients respond to these questions. So it can be difficult to measure the impact of cancer treatments across different subgroups.<br/><br/>"We want the questions we ask patients about their experiences to be reliable and accurate for all groups of people. That way we can use them more widely and with confidence in both research and clinical practice to measure the effectiveness of health care," explains Potosky. He and other investigators at Lombardi are working with an NIH funded group of research institutions to test questions that are used to measure the experiences of patients for their accuracy. Ultimately, Potosky hopes to optimize the medical care each individual receives.<br/><br/>Potosky was recruited to Lombardi in 2008 after a 21-year career at the National Cancer Institute (NCI). Is minic a chuirtear teiripí ailse in oiriúint ar an gcineál seo leigheas, go príomha, bunaithe ar shaintréithe flaithiúla chomhdhéanamh agus meall duine, ach cuireann sé i bhfáth gur cheart go gcuirfí fachtóirí riosca pearsanta agus comhshaoil eile san áireamh sa prástacht sin chomh maith le luachanna agus roghanna cultúrtha. Cuireann sé i bhfáth gur féidir le go leor fachtóirí cur le torthaí galar, lena n-áirítear tosca síceolaíochta, sóisialta, eacnamaíocha agus cultúrtha.<br/><br/>Is é an toradh atá ar níos mó ná bith-eolaíocht othair," a deir sé, ar chaighdeán an chúraim a sheachadtar sa phobal. Tá an-dóchasach go bhfuil dochtúirí agus lucht déanta polasaithe níos feasaí fós go mbíonn tionchar criticiúil ag an meascán seo de fhachtóirí ar mharthanas agus ar fholláine na n-othar, agus go gcuirfidh athchóiriú cúram sláinte agus níos mó staidéir taighde san áireamh an dearcadh níos leithne seo maidir le leigheas.<br/><br/>Déanann Potosky staidéar ar éifeachtacht agus ar fho-iarsmaí teiripe hormónach a fhorordú le haghaidh ailse phróstatach.Tá buntáistí fadtéarmacha na teiripe seo soiléir, ach tá rioscaí tromchúiseacha i gceist le briste cnámh, fadhbanna croí, agus diaibéiteas. Tá súil ag Potosky na rioscaí agus na tairbhí seo a chur in iúl go soiléir do na hothair agus dá gcuid dochtúirí, ionas gur féidir le gach fear rogha pearsanta a dhéanamh.<br/><br/>Ina chuid taighde eile, déanann Potosky suirbhéanna faoi fholláine an othair agus cuireann sé taithí ar fáil tar éis cóireála ailse. Tugtar "bearta toradh tuairisciú othar" ar bhealaí caighdeánaithe chun a n-eispéiris a thuairisciú le hairíonna agus le gnéithe eile dá gcáilíocht saoil. Mar sin féin, d’fhéadfadh tosca pearsanta cosúil le stádas socheacnamaíoch, cúlra cultúrtha, aois agus cine tionchar a imirt ar an gcaoi a bhfreagraíonn othair do na ceisteanna seo. Mar sin, d’fhéadfadh sé a bheith deacair tionchar na cóireálacha ailse a thomhas ar fud foghrúpaí éagsúla.<br/><br/>"Ba mhaith linn na ceisteanna a chur ar othair faoi a dtaithí a bheith iontaofa agus cruinn do gach grúpa daoine. Ar an mbealach sin is féidir linn iad a úsáid níos forleithne agus le muinín i dtaighde agus i gcleachtas cliniciúil araon chun éifeachtacht an chúraim sláinte a thomhas,” a deir Potosky. Tá sé féin agus imscrúdaitheoirí eile ag Lombardi ag obair le grúpa institiúidí taighde atá maoinithe ag NIH chun ceisteanna a úsáidtear chun taithí othar a thástáil dá gcruinneas. I ndeireadh na dála, tá súil ag Potosky an cúram leighis a bharrfheabhsú a fhaigheann gach duine.<br/><br/>Earcaíodh Potótsky go Lombardi in 2008 tar éis gairme 21 bliain ag an Institiúid Náisiúnta Ailse (NCI). Mar fhochéimí in Ollscoil Virginia, roghnaigh Potóky gan réamhluaite—i gcoinne mian a thuismitheoirí—ar mian leis go mbeadh an rud is mó sa Stair agus i nGnóthaí Eachtracha. Agus céim mháistreachta á saothrú aige san Airgeadas agus sa Bhainistíocht Sláinte ag Scoil Sláinte Johns Hopkins, fuair Potosky a ghrá d’eipidéimeacht, staidéar ar dhaonra, agus a chuid PhD i dtaighde Seirbhísí Sláinte (HSR) a thuill go luath. Thaitin an cur chuige córasach liom a raibh baint aige le heipidéimeolaíocht, agus theastaigh uaim é a chur i bhfeidhm chun staidéar a dhéanamh ar eipidéimeolaíocht a bhaineann le cúram ailse sa phobal i gcoitinne, ar a bhfuil APSS den chuid is mó faoi".<br/><br/>De réir mar a bhíonn an fócas ar leigheas níos pearsantaithe ina chuid níos dlúithe de chúram sláinte, creideann sé, "ní mór dúinn a dhéanamh amach conas cabhrú le daoine tuiscint a fháil ar an líon méadaitheach roghanna a bheidh acu agus muid ag foghlaim níos mó agus níos mó faoi bhitheolaíocht agus a idirghníomhaíochtaí le cóireálacha agus othair eile. Trí thaighde córasach a dhéanamh ar chúram ailse a sheachadadh i suíomhanna cleachtais pobail, is féidir linn a lán níos mó a fhoghlaim faoi na ranníocaíochtaí a bhaineann le fachtóirí córais bitheolaíochta, iompraíochta agus seachadta ar thorthaí ailse. Is faisnéis ríthábhachtach í seo atá riachtanach chun treoir a thabhairt maidir le cleachtas cliniciúil agus beartais cúram sláinte agus chun bonn eolais a chur faoi, agus chun an cumas atá ann an cúram a sheachadadh go deireadh, an ailse a chosc, bás ailse a laghdú, costais a ísliú, agus folláine agus caighdeán beatha na n-othar a fheabhsú." |
