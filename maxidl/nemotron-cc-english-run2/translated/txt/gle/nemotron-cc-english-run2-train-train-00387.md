| A mass kill is a generic term used to describe a form of communal hunting strategy like a desert kite or buffalo jump, that has the intent of killing dozens if not hundreds of ungulate animals all at once. Mass kills are a form of Traditional Ecological Knowledge that was practiced in different variations all over the world, beginning at least as long ago as the Upper Paleolithic period of 25,000 to 30,000 years ago.
