Wollstonecraft is best known for A Vindication of the Rights of Woman (1792), in which she argues that women are not naturally inferior to men, but appear to be only because they lack education. | Perkins Title II Overview<br/><br/>Perkins Title II funds partnerships of Local Educational Agencies (LEAs), BOCES and postsecondary education institutions for the development and operation of CTE programs consisting of at least the last two years of secondary education and at least two years of postsecondary education. Secondary level Title II programs provide technical skills education that prepares students to transition to postsecondary studies. Title II programs are also designed to:<br/><br/>strengthen curricular and other linkages between secondary and postsecondary institutions;<br/><br/>align school, college and community systems in these programs of study;<br/><br/>support strong academic performance;<br/><br/>promote informed and appropriate career choice and preparation;and<br/><br/>ensure that students gain the knowledge and skills needed for careers in emerging fields.<br/><br/>Secondary students who complete programs of study are able to receive postsecondary credit for college, and are better prepared to transition to associate or baccalaureate degree programs.<br/><br/>Title II programs are required to have accountability systems that are able to<br/><br/>meet the new performance reporting requirements of the law and,<br/><br/>document program impact<br/><br/>Title II technical preparation education can begin as early as ninth grade. Planned sequences of study in grades 9-12, 10-12 or 11-12 are closely aligned with two years of postsecondary study in a technical field, in associate degree or apprenticeship programs.
