This condition is also known by the name of "Military Neck."<br/><br/>The primary presenting features of Cervical Kyphosis are quite variable and depend on the severity of the condition. Neck pain is yet another feature of Cervical Kyphosis. There will also be resultant weakness in the grip as a result of this condition. Some individuals may also feel numbness and tingling in the upper extremities due to Cervical Kyphosis.<br/><br/>Can You Treat Cervical Kyphosis?<br/><br/>If Cervical Kyphosis is diagnosed early then it is a condition that can be completely cured. The treatment options for Cervical Kyphosis depend significantly on the extent and severity of the condition. Conservative treatment for cervical kyphosis are administered in cases where there is primarily neck pain and the affected individual is concerned about the cosmetic appearance.<br/><br/>Nonsurgical measures to treat cervical kyphosis include physical therapy to correct the deformity, a neck brace, and pain medications to control the pain. Surgery is reserved in cases where there is nerve compression as a result of the abnormal curvature of the cervical spine. They can write specific provisions<br/>and thereby doom their work to rapid obsolescence, or they can write<br/>general provisions, thereby allowing substantial discretion to the<br/>authoritative interpreters, who in our system are the judges. The U.S.<br/>Constitution is a mixture of specific and general provisions. Many of<br/>the specific provisions have stood the test of time well or have been<br/>amended without much fuss. This is especially true of the rules<br/>establishing the structure and procedures of Congress. Most of the<br/>specific provisions creating rights, however, have fared poorly. This creates flexibility<br/>in the face of unforeseen changes, but it creates the possibility of<br/>alternative interpretations, and this possibility is an embarrassment<br/>for a theory of judicial legitimacy that denies judges have any right<br/>to exercise discretion. A choice among semantically plausible<br/>interpretations of a text, in circumstances remote from those<br/>contemplated by its drafters, requires the exercise of discretion and<br/>the weighing of consequences. Reading is not a form of deduction;<br/>understanding requires a consideration of consequences. If I say, "I'll<br/>eat my hat," one reason why my listeners will "decode" the meaning of<br/>this statement in nonliteral fashion is that I couldn't eat a hat if I<br/>tried. The Constitution does not say, "Read me broadly," or, "Read me<br/>narrowly." Read<br/>broadly, it guarantees even the indigent the effective assistance of<br/>counsel. It becomes not just a negative right to be allowed to hire a<br/>lawyer but a positive right to demand the help of the government in<br/>financing one's defense if one cannot do it oneself. The process is also known as "legalization."<br/><br/>Once you receive the apostilled document, the Turkish government will want it translated into Turkish by a government-licensed translator, and the translation notarized by a Turkish noter (notary).<br/><br/>Firstly, the document is issued by a government official who is officially known by the entity providing the apostille. Once the government official signs the document, the entity providing the apostille checks the signature using signature cards on file, or through other means of verification. They then stamp the document with the apostille, certifying that the document did indeed come from the government official who issued it. If the country the document is being sent to is a signatory of the Apostille convention of 1961 (Turkey is a signatory), the apostille will be accepted as proof that the government document is legitimate.
