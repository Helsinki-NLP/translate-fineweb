| One of the largest national parks in Indonesia, Kerinci Seblat protects almost 14,000 sq kilometres of tropical rain forests in central western Sumatra running down the Barisan mountain range and its foothills and covering parts of four provinces. Terrains varies from lowland forests up to the peak of mighty Mt Kerinci at 3,805 m.<br/><br/>The national park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site as part of the Rainforest Heritage of Sumatra and one of the most important tiger reserves in the world.<br/><br/>The national park was formed from a collection of watershed protection forests (hutan lindung) and nature reserves and was established in 1982 although its boundaries were not legally formalised until the mid 1990s.<br/><br/>The park is dominated by the Barisan mountains. Scenery in many areas is very spectacular with active and dormant volcanoes including Mount Kerinci, at 3805m the highest mountain in Sumatra and Indonesia's highest active volcano. The national park forests protect the headwaters of some of Sumatra's most important rivers which flow from springs and peat swamps like Ladeh Panjang (Kerinci district) and Danau Kebut (Merangin district) high in the mountains, as well as many lakes and wetlands.<br/><br/>The Great Sumatra Fault runs through the centre of the national park and forms the densely populated Kerinci valley, which lies in the middle of and is surrounded by national park forests.<br/><br/>Kerinci Seblat is most famous amongst bird-watchers as the best place to see most of the highland Sumatran endemic bird species including the Schneider's Pitta, Salvadore's pheasant and Sumatran Cochoa, all presumed extinct for much of the 20th century before being rediscovered here. More than 375 species of bird have been recorded to date.<br/><br/>This is also the most important location in Sumatra for wild Sumatran Tiger and one of the 12 most important tiger reserves anywhere in the world although seeing wild tigers is unusual due both to the dense forest and the animals' shy nature. Other fauna includes elephants (best seen in national park forests in Bengkulu), clouded leopards, tapirs, sun bears and at least seven species of primate.<br/><br/>Forest edge farmers continue to report occasional sightings of the mysterious orang pendek, a large, bipedal cryptozoological primate resembling an orangutan (which are not recorded in Kerinci Seblat).<br/><br/>The national park also houses the biggest and the tallest flowers in the world, the monstrous, flesh red flower of the parasite Rafflesia arnoldi which can grow up to a metre in diameter, and is best searched for in the Bengkulu area of the park - ask for flowering information in Curup. In the southern part of Kerinci district hope to see the slightly smaller Rafflesia hasselti which is a vivid dark red in colour. The huge Amorphophallus titanum and Amorphophallus gigas are also present and can grow up to 4 metres in height. A remarkable flower at higher altitudes on Mt Kerinci and Mt Tujuh is the Javanese edelweiss Anaphalis javanica, which only grows on volcanoes. This shrub can reach more than two metres in height and is colored white-green because of its small hairs; the flowers are yellow with white. Numerous orchids are also found, most often flowering at the beginning of the rainy season in late September or October.<br/><br/>Rainfall is heaviest between October-December and from February-April while May-August is mainly dry but with some occasional rains, there are no major variations in seasonal temperature. Photographers may wish to avoid the period July-August as these dry months are often hazy. Because much of the park is above 700m in altitude, evenings and nights tend to be cool while in the high mountains temperatures may occasionally drop as low as 5C at night and so trekkers should be prepared for cool evenings<br/><br/>Despite the Jambi province having an area name Kerinci, the closest way to get there is actually from Padang. Most visitors arrive from Padang's Minangkabau International Airport which offers quickest access for the Kerinci area of the national park (seven hours). Jambi (capital of Jambi province) is 9 hours away from Kerinci. Bengkulu airport (domestic flights from Jakarta) is better for visits to North Bengkulu (elephants) and the Curup area of the national park (rafflesia).<br/><br/>You may want to call your hotel or homestay in advance to arrange for personal transport (you can hire a car, van, or bus) for your trip, however there are good 'Travel' minibus and Share Taxi services from Padang and Jambi and Bengkulu to district capitals which border the park. Padang to Kerinci (Sungaipenuh) or Bangko (Merangin district) is a six-seven hour bus drive, Jambi city to Bangko five hours, Bengkulu city to Curup three hours while it is four hours to the elephant sanctuary at Seblat from Bengkulu city.<br/><br/>You should bring a photocopy of your passport, which is to be given to the authorities to process your national park entry permit. Visitors planning to climb Mt Kerinci can get a photocopy made at the village of Kersik Tuo at the foot of the mountain. Photocopies of your passport and the visa section are also useful if staying in a village as your homestay must report visitors to the village headman or local police post<br/><br/>The price for entry to the national park as of February 2012 is 20,000 Rupiah per person. Buy tickets at the national park headquarters in Sungaipenuh, Kerinci, at Kersiktua, at Curup (near Bengkulu) or in Bangko if you are planning to visit Renah kemumu or the national park forests in Merangin district.<br/><br/>There are frequent travel minibus services between district capitals, usually leaving in the morning or evening. Angkot minibuses serve villages to and from district capitals. Roads in many areas are poorly maintained so travelling even a relatively short distance can take time, and especially so in the rainy season. It is possible to hire a car and driver in many of the district capitals - expect to pay about $40 a day plus petrol and driver's food.<br/><br/>You can also hire a self0drive motor bike or grab a ride on an ojek motorbike taxi - always negotiate a price before, ask help from your hotel if necessary.<br/><br/>Megaliths in the Kerinci valley and at remote and rarely visited Renah Kemumu village in Merangin district.<br/><br/>Lake Gunung Tujuh. South East Asia's highest crater lake and still surrounded by pristine forests and the marshlands of lake Bentau .<br/><br/>Rafflesia Arnoldii. The infamous Rafflesia Arnoldii can be found on the Bengkulu side of the park. Don't let the pretty appearance fool you because this is actually a corpse flower. But it only spews the horrid smell once in a while. (updated Feb 2017)<br/><br/>Mount Kerinci. The namesake mountain is the highest volcano in Indonesia at 3,805 meters (nearly 12,500 feet) above sea level. Climbing it takes at least 2 days and 1 night including the descent. There are 3 shelters throughout the path for camping. (updated Feb 2017)<br/><br/>The national park offers superb trekking and climbing opportunities for both novices and the more experienced as well as bird and wildlife watching whether just going for a walk for a day or a week long jungle expedition.<br/><br/>If staying in Sungaipenuh, ask the National Park office or your hotel to help you rent a car (motor bikes not advised!) for a Night Safari through the forests of Bukit Tapan: the road passes through dense forests and a night drive (leave about 10pm, return at 1-2am) gives a chance to experience tropical forest at night..and the chance to see some of the inhabitants (Sumatran tigers, Asiatic golden cat, sunbears, tapir, civet cats and rare serow antelopes are among the animals that have been spotted)<br/><br/>Go bird watching or just enjoy fantastic scenery from a dugout canoe in beautiful Danau (lake) Bentau, a stunning marshland area at the foot of Mt Tujuh. And yes, people do see tigers.<br/><br/>The Muara Imat-Birun area on the Kerinci-Merangin district borders is one of the best places to hope to see wild tigers or at least tiger signs as well as Rafflesia and Amorphophallus, rare orchids and good birdwatching. Ask at the national park offices.<br/><br/>The national park office has a range of t-shirts designed by young national park officers while exploring village and small town markets offers the chance to buy simple local handicrafts and woven baskets still routinely used by villagers. Never buy wildlife products or wild plants.<br/><br/>Food in the restaurants in the small towns around the national park is mainly Padang rice and a variety of meat, fish and vegetable dishes liberally spiced with chilli. If you do not like spicy food, ask for 'tidak pedas'. In Kerinci district look for restaurants offering 'dendeng batokok' - a grilled, finely sliced, smoked steak.<br/><br/>For visits to Mt Kerinci or Mt Tujuh stay at homestays in the village of Kersik Tuo. There are reasonable hotels and inns in Sungaipenuh, Bangko and Curup which are gateways to the national park and easily reached from provincial capitals. If you stay in a village as part of your adventure, please make a contribution to your host's expenses! In Birun, stay in pak Samsul's simple wooden house and learn all about tigers from this former Tiger ranger.<br/><br/>Even experienced climbers should avoid a solo climb of Mt Kerinci as there have been rare cases of disappearances and deaths primarily due to sudden changes in weather and the risk of poisonous gases from the crater. Forest treks involving camping overnight should be conducted with a reliable local guide - safer and much more rewarding. You can ask your local accommodation to arrange for a guide for you or request advice from national park officers. The national park's tiger protection rangers can be asked to act as guides for short forest treks during their leave periods (27-3 of each month) A highly recommended guide is Pak Ahmad, with around 30 years of experience bringing people up Mt Kerinci. Homestay Pak Subandi can also advise on guides. Pak Agustria, a former Tiger ranger based in Bangko is a good companion for trips to the remote and rarely visited Renah Kemumu, Jangkat and Sungai Tenang areas of the national park while pak Samsul, another former Tiger ranger, is a great guide to forests around Birun (between Kerinci and Merangin.<br/><br/>The homestays at the foot of Mt Kerinci also provide equipment rental, and your guide, or porters if you choose to hire any, will bring his own (basic) equipment and tent however in other areas you should bring a tent or ask your guide to organise camping tarpaulins etc.<br/><br/>The basic fee for an English-speaking guide at Mt Kerinci is about Rp 300,000 Rupiah per day, and 150,000 per porter however in other areas of the national park guide fees are less expensive - expect to pay about Rp150,000 a day for a highly experienced forest guide (though English-speaking forest guides will always charge a higher fee than forest edge villagers who know the forest better.) For deep forest adventures, buy a pocket dictionary so you can communicate more easily with your guide(s). Dictionaries don't often have the names of wild animals and birds - ask the park office to print out their tri-lingual list of wildlife (English, Indonesian and Latin names of species recorded)<br/><br/>It is also possible to explore the surrounding area in motorcycles, including the tea plantations. For real back country expeditions, in particular in national park edge villages in Merangin district, give yourself plenty of time and expect poor or very poor roads. Once away from Mt Kerinci or district capitals homestays or losmen are few and far between, if staying in villages, ask the village headman to organise accommodation. Look at the<br/>differences to determine what makes one book a winner, while the other is<br/>only mediocre.<br/><br/>Don't be afraid tackle<br/>difficult subject matter. Young<br/>people today are very savvy about what's going on in the world. Middle-grade fiction can include content<br/>on lying, bullying, jealousy, sibling rivalry, divorce, peer pressure,<br/>drug abuse, domestic violence, and suicide. These are all subjects many kids have<br/>had to deal with either directly or with family members, friends, or<br/>classmates. Just be sure to deal<br/>with these subjects in an appropriate manner.<br/><br/>Do make your characters<br/>slightly older than your targeted audience. Kids like to read about other kids a<br/>year or two older than they are. When I taught a writing class to 4th, 5th,<br/>and 6th grade students, I was always surprised by how old the<br/>characters were in the stories they chose to write. Growing up intrigues this age group.<br/><br/>Don't preach to your readers<br/>or talk down to them. They aren't<br/>toddlers or preschoolers. These are kids who know a lot about the world<br/>they live in and are exposed to so much through television and the<br/>Internet. Treat them as they want to be treated.<br/><br/>Do create three-dimensional<br/>characters. Know what your<br/>character looks like: keep a descriptive page handy so your character<br/>doesn't have red hair in chapter one and brown in chapter five. Know what<br/>they're good at: keep a list so you'll know if you're character is a<br/>computer whiz or can hit a baseball to outer field. Know what flaws your<br/>character has: make a chart listing things like easily angered, a poor<br/>student, too thin or overweight, etc.<br/>Know what quirks your character has: list things like a stutter,<br/>eye rubbing if she's tired, allergies which make him sneeze, an eye<br/>twitch, etc.<br/><br/>Don't forget to use the five<br/>senses: sight, smell, hear, taste, and touch. Bring your story to life with vivid<br/>descriptions: enticing aromas or horrific odors, owls hooting or cars<br/>crashing, Mom's yummy spaghetti cooking or the moldy sandwich the homeless<br/>guy pulls from the trash, and the soft velvet fur of a puppy or the slimy<br/>trail of a slug.<br/><br/>Do inject humor into your<br/>story, even those dealing with difficult topics. Move your story forward by<br/>creating obstacles for your characters to solve by themselves, without an<br/>adult's help, and be sure they solve those problems by the end of the<br/>story.<br/><br/>Do spend time with children<br/>in your targeted age group. Listen<br/>to their conversations. Observe their clothes and their behaviors. Without this first-hand information, it<br/>will be difficult for you to create a story and characters to which your<br/>readers will relate. Even if you don't have children in your immediate<br/>circle, volunteer at a school, community center, church, or scout group.<br/><br/>Don't ignore<br/>guidelines. After your story is<br/>completed do a search for publishers who might be interested in your<br/>work. Check out their catalogs to<br/>see what kinds of books they are publishing. Read their submission guidelines<br/>carefully. They are there for a<br/>reason. If they want Times New<br/>Roman, font size 12, be sure that's what you send to them. Edit your rough draft. Put it away. Edit it again. Ask unbiased people to read the<br/>manuscript and comment on it. Take<br/>their comments and make necessary adjustments. Let the manuscript sit for a week, then<br/>re-read it and make final edits. Be<br/>sure the manuscript is formatted to the specifications of the submissions'<br/>guidelines and send it off.<br/><br/>If<br/>you've followed my suggestions, you have a good chance of finding a publisher<br/>for your work, but remember, it takes patience, a well-written and grammatically<br/>correct manuscript, and above all, being in the right place, at the right time,<br/>with the right story. April 2013<br/><br/>Die Dos und Don'ts des Schreibens von Middle-Grade-Fiction<br/><br/>von Penny Lockwood<br/><br/>Das Schreiben von Romanen mittlerer Klasse unterscheidet sich vom Schreiben eines Bilderbuchs. Ich möchte einige der Dinge mit Ihnen teilen<br/>Ich habe gelernt, beides zu tun und nicht nur basierend auf meiner Schreibkarriere, sondern auch auf meiner Karriere als Redakteur für MuseItUp Publishing und Damnation.<br/>Publishing und ein Acquisitions-Editor für 4RV Publishing.<br/><br/>Beginnen Sie mit dem Lesen von Romanen, die von anderen Autoren geschrieben wurden.<br/>Achten Sie darauf, sowohl diejenigen zu lesen, die Auszeichnungen gewonnen haben, als auch solche mit schlechten Bewertungen. Schauen Sie sich die Unterschiede an, um festzustellen, was ein Buch zu einem Gewinner macht, während das andere nur mittelmäßig ist.<br/><br/>Haben Sie keine Angst, schwierige Themen anzugehen. Junge Menschen sind heute sehr versiert über das, was in der Welt vor sich geht. Mittelmäßige Fiktion kann Inhalte über Lügen, Mobbing, Eifersucht, Geschwisterrivalität, Scheidung, Gruppenzwang, Drogenmissbrauch, häusliche Gewalt und Selbstmord beinhalten. Dies sind alles Themen, mit denen sich viele Kinder entweder direkt oder mit Familienmitgliedern, Freunden oder Klassenkameraden auseinandersetzen mussten. Achten Sie darauf, sich mit diesen Themen angemessen auseinanderzusetzen.<br/><br/>Machen Sie Ihre Charaktere etwas älter als Ihre Zielgruppe. Kinder lesen gerne über andere Kinder ein oder zwei Jahre älter als sie sind. Als ich Schülern der 4., 5. und 6. Klasse eine Schreibstunde beibrachte, war ich immer überrascht, wie alt die Charaktere in den Geschichten waren, die sie schreiben wollten. Aufwachsen fasziniert diese Altersgruppe.<br/><br/>Predigen Sie Ihren Lesern nicht oder reden Sie nicht mit ihnen. Sie sind keine Kleinkinder oder Vorschulkinder. Dies sind Kinder, die viel über die Welt wissen, in der sie leben, und denen sie durch Fernsehen und Fernsehen so viel ausgesetzt sind.<br/>Internet. Behandle sie so, wie sie behandelt werden wollen.<br/><br/>Erstelle dreidimensionale Charaktere. Du solltest wissen, wie dein Charakter aussieht: Halte eine beschreibende Seite griffbereit, damit dein Charakter keine roten Haare in Kapitel 1 und braun in Kapitel 5 hat. Wissen Sie, was sie gut sind: Halten Sie eine Liste, so dass Sie wissen, ob Sie Charakter ist ein Computer whiz oder kann einen Baseball zu äußeren Feld schlagen. Wissen Sie, welche Fehler Ihr Charakter hat: Machen Sie ein Diagramm, in dem Dinge wie leicht verärgert, ein armer Schüler, zu dünn oder übergewichtig usw. aufgeführt sind.<br/>Wissen Sie, was Ihr Charakter hat: Listen Sie Dinge wie ein Stottern auf, reiben Sie die Augen, wenn sie müde ist, Allergien, die ihn niesen lassen, ein Augenzwinkern usw.<br/><br/>Vergessen Sie nicht, die fünf Sinne zu benutzen: Sehen, Riechen, Hören, Schmecken und Berühren. Bringen Sie Ihre Geschichte mit lebendigen Beschreibungen zum Leben: verlockende Aromen oder schreckliche Gerüche, Eulen husten oder Autos abstürzen, Mamas leckere Spaghetti-Küche oder das schimmelige Sandwich, das der Obdachlose aus dem Müll zieht, und das weiche Samtfell eines Welpen oder die schleimige Spur einer Schnecke.<br/><br/>Injizieren Sie Humor in Ihre Geschichte, auch für diejenigen, die sich mit schwierigen Themen befassen. Zum beispiel in ghost for rent, während sich unsere charaktere an die tatsache anpassen, dass sich ihre eltern trennen, neckt der bruder meiner hauptfigur sie, indem er gefälschte spinnen in ihr sandwich steckt und gefälschte würmer auf ihrem rücken.<br/><br/>Lassen Sie Ihre Charaktere nicht hängen. Bewegen Sie Ihre Geschichte voran, indem Sie Hindernisse für Ihre Charaktere schaffen, die sie ohne die Hilfe eines Erwachsenen selbst lösen können, und stellen Sie sicher, dass sie diese Probleme am Ende der Geschichte lösen.<br/><br/>Verbringen Sie Zeit mit Kindern in Ihrer Zielgruppe. Hören Sie sich ihre Gespräche an. Beobachten Sie ihre Kleidung und ihr Verhalten. Ohne diese Informationen aus erster Hand wird es für Sie schwierig sein, eine Geschichte und Charaktere zu erstellen, auf die sich Ihre Leser beziehen werden. Auch wenn Sie keine Kinder in Ihrem unmittelbaren Kreis haben, melden Sie sich freiwillig in einer Schule, einem Gemeindezentrum, einer Kirche oder einer Pfadfindergruppe.<br/><br/>Ignorieren Sie Richtlinien nicht. Nachdem Ihre Geschichte abgeschlossen ist, suchen Sie nach Verlagen, die an Ihrer Arbeit interessiert sein könnten. Schauen Sie sich ihre Kataloge an, um zu sehen, welche Arten von Büchern sie veröffentlichen. Lesen Sie ihre Einreichungsrichtlinien sorgfältig durch. Sie sind aus einem bestimmten Grund da. Wenn sie Times New<br/>Roman, Schriftgröße 12, stellen Sie sicher, dass es das ist, was Sie ihnen schicken. Bearbeiten Sie Ihren Rohentwurf. Legen Sie ihn weg. Bearbeiten Sie ihn erneut. Bitten Sie unvoreingenommene Leute, das Manuskript zu lesen und zu kommentieren. Nehmen Sie ihre Kommentare und nehmen Sie die notwendigen Anpassungen vor. Lassen Sie das Manuskript für eine Woche sitzen, dann lesen Sie es erneut und machen Sie endgültige Änderungen. Stellen Sie sicher, dass das Manuskript nach den Spezifikationen der Einreichungen formatiert ist.<br/>Richtlinien und senden Sie es ab.<br/><br/>Wenn Sie meinen Vorschlägen gefolgt sind, haben Sie eine gute Chance, einen Verleger für Ihre Arbeit zu finden, aber denken Sie daran, es braucht Geduld, ein gut geschriebenes und grammatikalisch korrektes Manuskript und vor allem, am richtigen Ort zu sein, zur richtigen Zeit, mit der richtigen Geschichte.
