| Description: SPI (Serial Peripheral Interface) is a synchronous serial interface technology proposed by Motorola. It is a high-speed, full-duplex, synchronous communication bus that occupies only four pins in the chip. Control and data transfer, widely used in EEPROM, Flash, RTC (real time clock), ADC (digital to analog converter), DSP (digital signal processor) and digital signal decoder.
