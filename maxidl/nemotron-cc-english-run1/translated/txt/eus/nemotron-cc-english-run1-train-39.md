They are single images with an arrow pointing to the relevant structure that needs to be identified and named. Answers are written in pen on an answer sheet.<br/><br/>This exam covers all body systems and imaging modalities. There will be some questions that state 'What is the normal variants' as well as 'identify what structure the arrow is pointing to'. Lying facedown in the water with your legs fully extended, propel forward with fast "flutter" kicks. Alternately bring one arm from the side of your body, out and over the water in front of you, and then back down into the water, pushing your hand down toward your feet. Since you won't be able to see where you are going, use the lane markers on each side of you so that you know where you are in the pool. Never perform this stroke in a crowded lane where you are likely to run into other people.<br/><br/>Breaststroke<br/>Although this is a slower stroke, it's still a great workout. Lying facedown, keep your body level at the surface. Think of how a frog jumps as you bend your knees toward your body, and then push them back out, straightening your legs. Begin with your arms in front of you, under the water, and then bring both hands out and around toward the body, making a circle under the water, and repeat. To breathe, lift your head out of the water as you pull your arms in. Pull your legs and arms in at the same time.<br/><br/>Equipment<br/>If you plan to swim regularly, you'll need some basic equipment for safety and comfort:<br/><br/>Well-fitting goggles will keep chlorine out of your eyes, decreasing irritation while allowing you to watch where you're going to avoid both people and walls.<br/><br/>A swim cap will keep your hair out of your face so you can focus on your workout, not your hairstyle.<br/><br/>A supportive bathing suit is essential to swimming.
