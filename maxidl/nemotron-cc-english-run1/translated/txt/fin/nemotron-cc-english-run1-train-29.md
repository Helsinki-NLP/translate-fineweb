It introduces young people to exciting, positive, challenging and enjoyable things to do in their free time. It encourages friendships amongst those taking part, and across the generations with adults who give up time to share skills and help organise the programme.<br/><br/>The Programme and Benefits<br/><br/>Anyone between the ages of 14 and 24 can take up the programme, regardless of gender, background or ability. All Awards must be completed by the participant's 25th Birthday. You must fill out a registration form and receive a record book before you can count any activities towards The Challenge.<br/><br/>The Benefits:<br/><br/>Friendship Achievement<br/><br/>Enjoyment Confidence<br/><br/>New skills Teamwork<br/><br/>Improved abilities Decision making<br/><br/>Excitement Time management<br/><br/>Wider horizons Local, national and international connections<br/><br/>How Does It Work?<br/><br/>Through a four section programme with three levels<br/>The levels are:<br/>• Bronze - open to all aged 14 and over and lasting at least 6 months<br/>• Silver - open to all aged 15 and over and lasting at least 12 months<br/>• Gold - open to all aged 16 and over and lasting at least 18 months<br/><br/>The sections are:<br/>• Service - giving help in the local community<br/>• Skills - covering almost any non physical hobby, skill or interest<br/>• Expeditions - training for, practising, planning and completing a journey on foot, horseback or by boat or cycle<br/>• Physical Recreation - sport, dance and fitness<br/>• Residential Project - for the Gold level only - spending 5 or more days on a purposeful project with new companions<br/><br/>How Is The Programme Organised In New Zealand?<br/><br/>There are approximately 700 organisations licensed to operate The Challenge. They are schools, voluntary youth organisations, and other community and welfare groups.<br/><br/>The Challenge can be run anywhere that young people get together, by anyone with an interest in developing young people or looking for a means to stretch and challenge them.<br/><br/>The Challenge in New Zealand is a not-for-profit organisation. It is also a franchise operation, licensing other organisations to run the programme.
