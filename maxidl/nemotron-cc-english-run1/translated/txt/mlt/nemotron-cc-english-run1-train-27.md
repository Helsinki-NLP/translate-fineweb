Generated with ClustalW.<br/><br/>A multiple sequence alignment (MSA) is a sequence alignment of three or more biological sequences, generally protein, DNA, or RNA. In general, the input set of query sequences are assumed to have an evolutionary relationship by which they share a lineage and are descended from a common ancestor. From the resulting MSA, sequence homology can be inferred and phylogenetic analysis can be conducted to assess the sequences' shared evolutionary origins. Visual depictions of the alignment as in the image at right illustrate mutation events such as point mutations (single amino acid or nucleotide changes) that appear as differing characters in a single alignment column, and insertion or deletion mutations (or indels) that appear as gaps in one or more of the sequences in the alignment. Multiple sequence alignment is often used to assess sequence conservation of protein domains, tertiary and secondary structures, and even individual amino acids or nucleotides.<br/><br/>Multiple sequence alignment also refers to the process of aligning such a sequence set. Because three or more sequences of biologically relevant length are nearly impossible to align by hand, computational algorithms are used to produce and analyze the alignments. MSAs require more sophisticated methodologies than pairwise alignment because they are more computationally complex to produce. Most multiple sequence alignment programs use heuristic methods rather than global optimization because identifying the optimal alignment between more than a few sequences of moderate length is prohibitively computationally expensive.<br/><br/>Dynamic programming and computational complexity<br/><br/>The most direct method for producing an MSA uses the dynamic programming technique to identify the globally optimal alignment solution. For proteins, this method usually involves two sets of parameters: a gap penalty and a substitution matrix assigning scores or probabilities to the alignment of each possible pair of amino acids based on the similarity of the amino acids' chemical properties and the evolutionary probability of the mutation. For nucleotide sequences a substitution matrix can be used, but since there are only four possible standard characters per sequence and the individual nucleotides do not typically differ much in substitution probability, the parameters for DNA and RNA sequences usually consist of a gap penalty, a positive score for character matches, and a negative score for mismatches.<br/><br/>For n individual sequences, the method requires constructing the n-dimensional equivalent of the matrix formed in standard pairwise dynamic programming. The search space thus increases exponentially with increasing n and is also strongly dependent on sequence length. To find the global optimum for n sequences this way has been shown to be an NP-complete problem.[1][2] Methods to reduce the search space by first performing pairwise dynamic programming on each pair of sequences in the query set and searching only the solution space near these results (effectively finding the intersection between local paths immediately surrounding each pairwise optimum solution) render the dynamic programming technique more efficient. The so-called "sum of pairs" method has been implemented in the software package MSA, but it is still impractical for many MSA applications that require the simultaneous alignment of dozens or even a few hundred sequences. Dynamic programming methods are now used only when an extremely high-quality alignment of a small number of sequences is needed, and as a benchmarking standard in evaluating new or refined heuristic techniques.<br/><br/>Progressive alignment construction<br/><br/>One method of performing a heuristic alignment search is the progressive technique (also known as the hierarchical or tree method) that builds up a final MSA by first performing a series of pairwise alignments on successively less closely related sequences. Such methods begin by aligning the two most closely related sequences first and then successively aligning the next most closely related sequence in the query set to the alignment produced in the previous step. The initial "most related" pair is determined by an efficient clustering method such as neighbor-joining based on a simple heuristic search of the query set with a tool like FASTA. Progressive techniques therefore automatically construct a phylogenetic tree as well as an alignment.<br/><br/>One major limitation of progressive methods is their heavy dependence on the initial assignment of relatedness and on the quality of the initial alignment. The methods are thus sensitive as well to the distribution of sequences in the query set; performance improves when relatedness among query sequences is a relatively smooth gradient rather than distantly separated clusters. Performance also degrades significantly when all of the sequences in the set are rather distantly related, because inaccuracies in the initial alignment are then more likely. Most modern progressive methods modify their scoring function with a secondary weighting function that assigns scaling factors to individual members of the query set in a nonlinear fashion based on their phylogenetic distance from their nearest neighbors. Judicious choice of weighting can aid in evaluating relatedness and mitigate the effects of relatively poor initial alignments early in the progression.<br/><br/>Progressive alignment methods are efficient enough to implement on a large scale for many sequences and are often run on publicly accessible web servers so users need not locally install the applications of interest. A very popular progressive alignment method is the Clustal family,[3] especially the weighted variant ClustalW[4] to which access is provided by a large number of web portals including GenomeNet, EBI, and EMBNet. Different portals or implementations can vary in user interface and make different parameters accessible to the user. Clustal is used extensively for phylogenetic tree construction and as input for protein structure prediction by homology modeling.<br/><br/>Another common progressive alignment method called T-Coffee[5] is slower than Clustal and its derivatives but generally produces more accurate alignments for distantly related sequence sets. It uses the output from Clustal as well as another local alignment program LALIGN, which finds multiple regions of local alignment between two sequences. L-għażla ġudizzjarja tal-piż tista' tgħin fl-evalwazzjoni tar-relazzjoni u ittaffi l-effetti ta' allinjamenti inizjali relattivament baxxi kmieni fil-progress.<br/><br/>Il-metodi progressivi ta' allinjament huma effiċjenti biżżejjed biex jimplimentaw fuq skala kbira għal ħafna sekwenzi u ħafna drabi huma operati fuq servers tal-web aċċessibbli pubblikament sabiex l-utenti ma jkollhomx għalfejn jinstallaw l-applikazzjonijiet tal-interess lokalment. Hija tuża l-output mill-Clutal kif ukoll programm ieħor ta' allinjament lokali LALIGN, li jsib reġjuni multipli ta' allinjament lokali bejn żewġ sekwenzi. L-allinjament li jirriżulta u s-siġra foloġikali jintużaw bħala gwida biex jiġu prodotti fatturi ta' piż ġodda u aktar preċiżi.<br/><br/>Minħabba li l-metodi progressivi huma eristiċi li mhumiex garantiti biex jevolvu għal ottimu globali, il-kwalità tal-allinjament tista' tkun diffiċli biex tiġi evalwata u s-sinifikat bijoloġiku reali tagħhom jista' jkun ta' ostaklu. Wieħed mill-metodi progressivi huwa tant dipendenti fuq allinjament inizjali ta' kwalità għolja huwa l-fatt li dawn l-allinjamenti huma dejjem inkorporati fir-riżultat finali - jiġifieri, ladarba sekwenza tkun ġiet allinjata mal-MSA, l-allinjament tagħha ma jiġix ikkunsidrat aktar. Din l-approssimazzjoni ttejjeb l-effiċjenza fl-ispiża tal-preċiżjoni. B'kuntrast ma' dan, il-metodi iterattivi jistgħu jirritornaw biex qabel jiġu kkalkulati l-allinjamenti tal-pari jew is-sottoATSjiet jinkorporaw subsettijiet tas-sekwenza tal-mistoqsijiet bħala mezz ta' ottimizzazzjoni ta' funzjoni oġġettiva ġenerali bħas-sejba ta' punteġġ ta' allinjament ta' kwalità għolja.<br/><br/>Varjetà ta' metodi ta' imitazzjoni sostanzjalment differenti ġew implimentati u saru disponibbli f'pakketti ta' softwer; reviżjonijiet u paraguni kienu utli iżda ġeneralment iżommu lura milli jagħżlu teknika "aħjar".[7] Il-pakkett ta' softwer PRN/RP juża algoritmu tat-tixbit tal-għoljiet biex jottimizza l-punteġġ ta' allinjament tal-MSA[8] tiegħu u jikkoreġi b'mod iterattiv kemm il-piżijiet tal-allinjament kif ukoll ir-reġjuni tal-"Gappy" lokalment diverġenti jew li qed jikbru tal-MSA.[9] PRP twettaq l-aħjar meta r-raffinar ta' allinjament li qabel kienet mibnija b'metodu aktar mgħaġġel. L-allinjament ta' motivi individwali mbagħad jinkiseb b'rappreżentazzjoni ta' matriċi simili għal plot ta' dotmatriċi f'allinjament pareżiv. Metodu alternattiv li juża l-allinjamenti lokali veloċi bħala punti ta' ankra jew "żrieragħ" għal proċedura ta' allinjament globali aktar bil-mod huwa implimentat fis-suite CHAOS/DALIGN.[10].<br/><br/>It-tielet metodu popolari bbażat fuq it-tema msejjaħ MUSTLE (allinjament ta' sekwenza multipla permezz ta' log-aspettazzjoni) jtejjeb il-metodi progressivi b'miżura ta' distanza aktar preċiża biex tiġi vvalutata r-relazzjoni ta' żewġ sekwenzi.[11] Il-miżura tad-distanza tiġi aġġornata bejn l-istadji ta' tisqija (għalkemm, fil-forma oriġinali tagħha, MUSTLE fih biss 2-3 ekwazzjonijiet skont jekk ir-riforniment kienx permess).<br/><br/>Mudelli moħbija Markov<br/><br/>Mudelli moħbija Markov huma mudelli probabbilistika li jistgħu jassenjaw il-probabbiltàjiet possibbli kollha ta' kombinazzjonijiet, logħbiet, u diskrepanzi biex jiġi ddeterminat l-iktar MSCA probabbli jew sett ta' MSCAs possibbli. L-MMR jistgħu jipproduċu output uniku fl-ogħla punteġġ iżda jistgħu wkoll jiġġeneraw familja ta' allinjamenti possibbli li mbagħad jistgħu jiġu evalwati għas-sinifikat bijoloġiku. Minħabba li l-MMR huma problematiċi, dawn ma jipproduċux l-istess soluzzjoni kull darba li jimxu fuq l-istess sett ta' data; għalhekk ma jistgħux jiġu ggarantiti li konverġenti b'mod ottimali. L-MMR jistgħu jipproduċu kemm l-allinjamenti globali kif ukoll lokali. Għalkemm il-metodi bbażati fuq l-HM ġew żviluppati relattivament reċentement, huma joffru titjib sinifikanti fil-veloċità komputazzjonali, speċjalment għal sekwenzi li fihom reġjuni li jikkoinċidu.[9]<br/><br/>Metodi tipiċi bbażati fuq HMM jaħdmu billi jirrappreżentaw MSCA bħala forma ta' grafika aċiklika diretta magħrufa bħala graff parzjali tal-ordni, li jikkonsisti f'serje ta' nodi li jirrappreżentaw entrati possibbli fil-kolonni ta' SSA. F'din ir-rappreżentazzjoni kolonna li tkun assolutament kkonservata (jiġifieri, li s-sekwenzi kollha fl-MSA jaqsmu karattru partikolari f'pożizzjoni partikolari) tiġi kkodifikata bħala nodu wieħed b'ħafna konnessjonijiet li jistgħu jseħħu peress li hemm karattri possibbli fil-kolonna li jmiss tal-allinjament. Fit-termini ta' mudell ta' Markov tipiku, l-istati osservati huma l-kolonni ta' allinjament individwali u l-istati moħbija li jirrappreżentaw is-sekwenza ta' antenati preżunta minn fejn is-sekwenzi ta' domanda huma ipoteżi li naqsu. Varjant ta' tiftix effiċjenti tal-metodu ta' programmazzjoni dinamiku, magħruf bħala l-algoritmu Vitbi, ġeneralment jintuża biex jallinja l-MSA li qed tikber fuq is-sekwenza li jmiss fil-mistoqsija stabbilita biex tipproduċi ATS ġdida.[12] Dan huwa distint minn metodi ta' allinjament progressivi minħabba li l-allinjament tas-sekwenzi preċedenti huwa aġġornat f'kull żieda ta' sekwenza ġdida. Madankollu, bħal metodi progressivi, din it-teknika tista' tiġi influwenzata mill-ordni li fiha s-sekwenzi fil-mistoqsijiet jiġu integrati fl-allinjament, speċjalment meta s-sekwenzi jkunu relatati b'mod kostanti.[9]<br/><br/>Diversi programmi ta' softwer huma disponibbli fejn varjanti ta' metodi bbażati fuq l-HM ġew implimentati u li huma nnotati għall-iskala u l-effiċjenza tagħhom, għalkemm l-użu xieraq ta' metodu HMM huwa aktar kumpless milli bl-użu ta' metodi progressivi aktar komuni.
