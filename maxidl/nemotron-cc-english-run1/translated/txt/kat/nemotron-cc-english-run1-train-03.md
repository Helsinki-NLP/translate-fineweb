So how do you decide which one to buy? If you believe most advertising, only one figure matters, the number of megapixels. But what are they?<br/><br/>Put simply, megapixels are the millions of dots on a light-sensitive chip which a digital camera uses instead of film. There is a parallel with printing, in that if you look at the pictures in this newspaper you will see that they are made up of dots. This is "resolution".<br/><br/>That does not mean, however, that the number of megapixels is all you should consider when buying a digital camera; that would be the same as saying that the quality of a non-digital camera was defined simply by the type of film it could use.
