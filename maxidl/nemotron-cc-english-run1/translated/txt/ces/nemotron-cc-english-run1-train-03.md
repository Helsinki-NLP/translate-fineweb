But what are they?<br/><br/>Put simply, megapixels are the millions of dots on a light-sensitive chip which a digital camera uses instead of film. This is "resolution".<br/><br/>That does not mean, however, that the number of megapixels is all you should consider when buying a digital camera; that would be the same as saying that the quality of a non-digital camera was defined simply by the type of film it could use. The important point is it should be possible to take pictures as good with the cheapest of these three cameras as with the most expensive.
