He felt it was inferior, "…compared to space, thundering with images and crammed with sounds." In 1962, cinematographer – and for many the grandfather of VR – Morton Heilig built the Sensorama. User's would sit in front of the bulky device, and put their faces into a 'Telesphere Mask'. Once comfortable, they would be exposed to a wide-angle screen displaying stereoscopic 3-D images of such environments as a busy street. The experience was usually a simulation of riding a bike or motorcycle, replete with tilting seat and vents that would release puffs of wind, and typical street aromas.<br/><br/>It's all a long way from the head mounted devices of today, many of which can be paired with chairs and body suits that simulate pressure and impact for maximum sensory immersion.
