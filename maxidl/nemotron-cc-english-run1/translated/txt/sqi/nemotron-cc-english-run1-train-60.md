| This article looks into novels that depict historical events from a child's perspective. Herefore, two contemporary lusophone novels from outside Europe are analyzed. The question is why complex topics are presented by a narrative voice that is limited. For instance, we find functions such as attributing innocence to the protagonist thus increasing the dramatic hero's fall, sidestepping censorship by construing a supposedly naïve position, or the possibility of reading the text as a parable on a country which either loses its innocence or is at the onset of some sort of development. Furthermore, I will point out narratological strategies that are used to make up for potential limitations in this narrative situation. | Ky artikull hedh vështrim në romane që përshkruajnë ngjarje historike nga perspektiva e një fëmije.Prandaj, analizohen dy romane bashkëkohore të luksit nga jashtë Europës.
