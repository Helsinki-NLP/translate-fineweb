| Robin Adair is a traditional Irish (sometimes identified as Scottish) song with lyrics written by Lady Caroline Keppel. He was a surgeon-colonel in the British army, and declined a baronetcy. Though her family disapproved of the match because of his lower status, Lady Caroline Keppel (c. 1734–1769)[3] married him. In response to her family, she wrote this song in the 1750s. The couple had a son named Robert, who was part of the British diplomatic corps. The melody of the song may have been written by Charles Coffey.
