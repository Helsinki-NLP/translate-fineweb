At around 4PM, Captain Ernest McSorley radioed a trailing vessel, the SS Arthur Anderson, to say he was taking on water and his radar had failed. He was looking for guidance to Whitefish Bay, where he hoped to wait out the storm.<br/><br/>At 7:10 PM, McSorley radioed to say the crew of the Fitzgerald were "holding our own." Minutes later, the ship went down. No distress signal was sent and the wreckage wouldn't be found for another four days.
