| The Ottoman Empire started its westward expansion into Europe in the middle of the 14th century. More than forty-five thousand Ottoman troops, including four pashas, have been reported as part of the Ottoman casualties. At the same time, the Ottoman Empire started sea campaigns, as early as 1423, when it waged a seven year war with the Venetian Republic over maritime control of the Aegean and the Adriatic. It has been argued that their resiliency halted their advance of the Eastern flank of the Western Civilization, thereby saving the Italian peninsula from Ottoman conquest.<br/><br/>The Kingdom of Hungary, which at the time spanned the area from Croatia in the west to Transylvania in the east, was also gravely impacted by Ottoman conquest.
